Hacker Rank Timeline

2009

August 2009

Interview Street launched as a platform for mock interviews

December 2009

10-15 interviews bring us in about a $150 revenue.

2010

January 2010

We have a long conversation at the beach discussing how to get more users

March 2010

Went to almost every college in South India to stick posters and give lectures on CS/Algorithms to gain traction. We see a feeble increase in the response.

April 2010

Applied to YC. Decided to pivot. We and our friends at Amazon and Google were tasked with conducting employee interviews. It wasn't scalable and didn't go anywhere.

May 2010

Rejected by YC. Pivoted to pair people from India who were applying for a US Masters degree with alums to conduct mock interviews.

August 2010

A bit of growth, but turns out alums don't want to charge money. So much for our business model, so another pivot...

October 2010

Tried to set up mock interviews for applicants to top tier MBA programs with alums. Applied for YC again for W11 batch.

November 2010

Got rejected from YC again. We were almost bankrupt. Had roughly $400 in the bank account.

December 2010

Another beach talk. We decide to build a web-based app to solve the problem of screening programmers.

2011

January 2011

Locked ourselves in a room for 10 days and created the first version of our tool to help companies screen programmers. Pitched hard to companied. Vivek pitched to 3 companies per day and Hari coded all night.

March 2011

We got a few customers using our product, the biggest name being Amazon. Applied to YC again...

April 2011

We get a call from Facebook wanting to try out product. Two days later YC says they want to meet us, and soon after a VC firm wants to invest.

May 2011

The VC backs out. Vivek flies to CA for the 10 minute interview. "I attended the interview fearlessly with an attitude like I've got nothing to lose so let me be genu ine and straightforward" 2 hours later we found out we got into YC.

July 2011

After getting more US customers, we launch challenges to help companies hire programmers in addition to screening them.

August 2011

Launched challenges to big support, grew to a team of six. YC demo day.

September 2011

Pitched to several VCs and angels and backed by Vinod Khosla. Launched CodeSprint-1 with 3.5k programmers

October 2011

Returned to India, got a new office space.

November 2011

Several members of our small team leave to start new startups. Back to 2 founders.

2012

January 2012

CodeSprint 2 launches with 5k programmers. Started hiring programmers in India. Vivek moves to the US

March 2012

We started conducting weekly Codesprints and matching programmers with companies, but started to realize that many top coders don't need job placement services. Grew to a team of six in India.

April 2012

Several members of our small team leave to start new startups. Back to 2 founders.
