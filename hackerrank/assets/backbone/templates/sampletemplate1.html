Introduction

Colors

Green & Alt-Green: These colors are reserved for links and headers requiring primary attention. Use sparingly, so as to keep it from conflicting with other primary actions and elements on the page. $alt-Green should be used for text while $greenis reserved for large blocks.

Blue & Alt-Blue: Blue is our secondary branding color. Use it for filters and explanatory text, such as when toggling options in the settings panel. As with our green, $alt-blue should be used for text and $blue for larger blocks.

Bright Orange: Bright Orange is generally used to distinguish errors, warnings, or other elements requiring attention.

Yellow: Yellow is used for highlights, including selected text. Use it for emphasis in addition to em and strong tags.

Our Grays: We use several colors across the navy/gray spectrum. $navy is the color of text throughout the app. Use $alt-grey sparingly for asides, meta text, or other non-crucial text. Always check your text for accessibility against the W3C standards

Hints

To force a centered button without a fixed width, use the class .text-center on a surrounding block element.

<p class="text-center"><a href="#" class="btn btn-primary">Centered Button</a></p>

Centered Button

If the button has a fixed width, use the class .block-center to center it. This goes for any block element, or element with a fixed width.

<p class="block-center"><a href="#" class="btn btn-primary">300px button</a></p>
